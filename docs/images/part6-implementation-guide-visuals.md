## Visual ID: 17-Figure-17.2
**Type**: Comparison Matrix
**Title**: Implementation Approach Selection Matrix
**Chapter Reference**: Chapter 17: Implementation Roadmap

### Purpose Description
Helps organizations select the most appropriate implementation approach (Big Bang, Phased Rollout, or Pilot-Based) based on their context, resources, urgency, and risk tolerance.

### Content Description
**Structure**:
- Three-column comparison matrix
- Rows covering key decision factors
- Visual indicators for suitability
- Recommendations and risk levels
- Example scenarios for each approach

**Visual Flow**:
- Left to right: Most aggressive to most conservative
- Top to bottom: Decision factors to recommendations
- Color-coded suitability ratings

**Key Elements**:

**Column Headers**:
1. **Big Bang Approach**
   - Icon: Lightning bolt or explosion
   - Color theme: #FF6B35 (Accent Orange)
   - Subtitle: "Comprehensive, Simultaneous Implementation"

2. **Phased Rollout Approach**
   - Icon: Stairs or progressive steps
   - Color theme: #0066CC (Primary Blue)
   - Subtitle: "Incremental, Staged Implementation"

3. **Pilot-Based Approach**
   - Icon: Test tube or experiment
   - Color theme: #28A745 (Success Green)
   - Subtitle: "Prove Value, Then Expand"

**Row Categories and Content**:

**1. Implementation Timeline**
- Big Bang: 3-6 months
- Phased: 12-24 months
- Pilot-Based: 18-30 months
- Indicator: Calendar icons showing relative timeframes

**2. Resource Requirements**
- Big Bang: HIGH - Significant upfront investment
  - Multiple FTEs simultaneously
  - Compressed training schedule
  - Intensive support period
- Phased: MODERATE - Spread over longer period
  - Growing resource pool
  - Staggered training
  - Manageable support loads
- Pilot-Based: LOW-MODERATE - Initial investment small
  - Start with 1-2 practitioners
  - Grow based on proof
  - Controlled expansion
- Indicator: Resource meter (High/Med/Low)

**3. Risk Level**
- Big Bang: HIGH
  - All-or-nothing approach
  - Limited learning before scale
  - High visibility of failures
- Phased: MODERATE
  - Risk distributed over time
  - Early phases inform later
  - Manageable scope per phase
- Pilot-Based: LOW
  - Contained initial risk
  - Prove before expanding
  - Can pause if issues arise
- Indicator: Risk thermometer (Red/Yellow/Green)

**4. Executive Support Required**
- Big Bang: CRITICAL - Must be strong from start
  - Significant commitment needed
  - Immediate enterprise visibility
  - No time to build confidence
- Phased: MODERATE - Can grow over time
  - Initial commitment moderate
  - Support builds with success
  - Expanding executive engagement
- Pilot-Based: LOW INITIALLY - Build through proof
  - Minimal initial commitment
  - Success builds support
  - Confidence grows with results
- Indicator: Support level bars

**5. Organizational Readiness**
- Big Bang: HIGH READINESS REQUIRED
  - Change-positive culture
  - Clear OCM need recognized
  - Resources available
  - Urgency exists
- Phased: MODERATE READINESS
  - Some OCM awareness
  - Willingness to learn
  - Growing capabilities
  - Patient stakeholders
- Pilot-Based: LOW READINESS OK
  - Skepticism acceptable
  - Learning orientation
  - Prove-it-first culture
  - Time available
- Indicator: Readiness gauge

**6. Benefits Realization**
- Big Bang: FASTEST - If successful
  - Immediate full capability
  - Quick enterprise adoption
  - Fast benefits if works
- Phased: GRADUAL - Incremental value
  - Early areas see benefits soon
  - Portfolio of successes builds
  - Sustained momentum
- Pilot-Based: DELAYED - Slow start
  - Pilot benefits limited
  - Expansion takes time
  - Full benefits delayed
- Indicator: Value curve over time graph

**7. Learning & Adaptation**
- Big Bang: LIMITED - Must get it right first time
  - Little opportunity to adjust
  - Mistakes impact widely
  - Post-mortem learning only
- Phased: GOOD - Learn between phases
  - Each phase informs next
  - Continuous refinement
  - Adaptive approach
- Pilot-Based: EXCELLENT - Extensive learning
  - Deep learning from pilots
  - Refinement before scale
  - Risk mitigation built in
- Indicator: Learning curve

**8. Organizational Change Capacity**
- Big Bang: MUST BE HIGH
  - Absorbs significant capacity
  - Competes with other changes
  - Risk of saturation
- Phased: MANAGEABLE
  - Distributed impact
  - Can fit with other initiatives
  - Controlled burn
- Pilot-Based: MINIMAL INITIAL
  - Limited early impact
  - Grow as capacity available
  - Low saturation risk
- Indicator: Capacity gauge

**Big Bang Best When**:
- âœ“ Strong, committed executive sponsorship
- âœ“ Urgent business need (regulatory, competitive)
- âœ“ Resources available immediately
- âœ“ Change-ready culture
- âœ“ Clear OCM value recognition
- âœ“ High organizational change capacity

**Phased Best When**:
- âœ“ Moderate executive support, can grow
- âœ“ Time available for staged approach
- âœ“ Resources can be developed gradually
- âœ“ Learning between phases valued
- âœ“ Normal change capacity
- âœ“ Want to balance speed with risk
- Example: "Transform OCM over 2 years as part of ITSM maturity journey"
- NOTE: "**MOST COMMON APPROACH - Recommended for most organizations**"

**Pilot Best When**:
- âœ“ Executive support must be proven
- âœ“ Skepticism about OCM value exists
- âœ“ Limited initial resources available
- âœ“ Time available to build confidence
- âœ“ Learning culture values proof
- âœ“ Can afford slow start
- Example: "Demonstrate OCM ROI on 2-3 projects before seeking enterprise funding"

**Decision Framework** (Bottom section):

**Choose Big Bang if you answer YES to most**:
- Do you have strong executive sponsorship NOW?
- Is there urgent business need?
- Are resources available immediately?
- Can you accept high initial risk?
- Is your organization change-ready?

**Choose Phased if you answer YES to most**:
- Do you have moderate support that can grow?
- Is 18-24 month timeline acceptable?
- Do you want to balance speed and risk?
- Can you build capability incrementally?
- Do you want learning between phases?

**Choose Pilot if you answer YES to most**:
- Must you prove value before commitment?
- Do you have limited initial resources?
- Is slow start acceptable?
- Is there skepticism to overcome?
- Do you have 2+ years available?

**Hybrid Approaches** (Note):
- Organizations may combine approaches
- Example: Pilot in one division, then phased enterprise rollout
- Example: Big bang methodology definition, phased coverage expansion
- Flexibility is key to success
