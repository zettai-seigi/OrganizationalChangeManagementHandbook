## Welcome

Welcome to the **Organizational Change Management (OCM) Handbook**, a comprehensive guide designed to help IT professionals, change managers, and organizational leaders successfully navigate the people side of change within IT Service Management (ITSM) environments.

This handbook provides practical guidance, frameworks, and best practices for managing organizational change effectively, ensuring that people successfully adopt, utilize, and realize value from IT transformations.

---

## What is Organizational Change Management?

**Organizational Change Management (OCM)** is the systematic approach to helping individuals, teams, and organizations transition from a current state to a desired future state by addressing the human aspects of change.

Within ITSM, OCM focuses on:
- Ensuring stakeholders understand why change is happening
- Preparing and training users to adopt new systems and processes
- Identifying and addressing resistance proactively
- Engaging leadership as active sponsors
- Measuring and sustaining change adoption over time

## Key Framework Elements

### The ADKAR Model

The ADKAR model is a foundational framework for individual change:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **A**wareness | Understanding why change is needed |
| **D**esire | Personal motivation to support change |
| **K**nowledge | Understanding how to change |
| **A**bility | Skills and behaviors to implement change |
| **R**einforcement | Sustaining change over time |

### 8 Critical Success Factors (CSFs)

| CSF # | Name | Category |
|-------|------|----------|
| 1 | Executive Sponsorship and Commitment | Leadership |
| 2 | Clear Vision and Case for Change | Foundation |
| 3 | Stakeholder Engagement and Involvement | People |
| 4 | Effective Communication | Communication |
| 5 | Training and Capability Building | Enablement |
| 6 | Change Agent Network | Structure |
| 7 | Resistance Management | Risk |
| 8 | Measurement and Reinforcement | Sustainability |

### 6 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

1. **Awareness Level** - % of stakeholders aware of change (target: 100%)
2. **Adoption Rate** - % of users actively using new processes (target: ≥85%)
3. **Proficiency Level** - % meeting competency standards (target: ≥80%)
4. **Resistance Level** - % showing active resistance (target: ≤10%)
5. **Sponsor Engagement Score** - Leadership participation metrics
6. **Sustainment Rate** - Maintained adoption after 6 months (target: ≥90%)

### 5 Maturity Levels

| Level | Name | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 1 | Ad-hoc | No formal OCM process |
| 2 | Emerging | Basic OCM on some projects |
| 3 | Defined | Standardized OCM methodology |
| 4 | Managed | Metrics-driven OCM across organization |
| 5 | Optimized | Continuous improvement, change-ready culture |
