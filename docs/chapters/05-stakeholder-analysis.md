# Chapter 5: Stakeholder Analysis and Engagement

## Learning Objectives

After completing this chapter, you will be able to:
- Identify and categorize stakeholders systematically
- Create stakeholder maps and influence diagrams
- Develop targeted engagement strategies
- Manage stakeholder relationships throughout change

---

## Stakeholder Identification

### Stakeholder Categories

| Category | Definition | Examples |
|----------|------------|----------|
| **Sponsors** | Authorize and fund the change | Executives, steering committee |
| **Impacted** | Daily work affected | End users, process owners |
| **Influencers** | Shape opinions and decisions | Managers, informal leaders |
| **Enablers** | Provide support/resources | IT, HR, Training |
| **Interested** | Have stake in outcomes | Customers, partners |

---

### Stakeholder Assessment Matrix

| Stakeholder | Impact | Influence | Current State | Desired State | Gap |
|-------------|--------|-----------|---------------|---------------|-----|
| Finance Team | High | Medium | Resistant | Supportive | Large |
| IT Support | Medium | Medium | Neutral | Engaged | Medium |
| End Users | High | Low | Unaware | Proficient | Large |

---

## Engagement Strategies

| Strategy | When to Use | Activities |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Inform** | Low impact, low influence | Newsletters, updates |
| **Consult** | Need input, medium influence | Surveys, focus groups |
| **Involve** | High impact, medium influence | Workshops, pilots |
| **Partner** | High impact, high influence | Co-design, decision-making |

---

## Key Takeaways

- Systematic stakeholder analysis is foundational to OCM success
- Different stakeholders require different engagement approaches
- Engagement must be maintained throughout the change lifecycle
- Stakeholder positions can shift - continuous monitoring is essential
