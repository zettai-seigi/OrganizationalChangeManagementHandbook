# Chapter 9: Training and Capability Building

## Learning Objectives

After completing this chapter, you will be able to:
- Design role-based training strategies
- Develop effective training content
- Deliver training using multiple modalities
- Evaluate training effectiveness

---

## Training Strategy Development

### Training Design Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Role-based** | Tailored to specific job needs |
| **Just-in-time** | Delivered close to when needed |
| **Blended** | Multiple methods combined |
| **Practice-focused** | Hands-on application |
| **Reinforced** | Followed by support |

---

## Training Delivery Methods

| Method | Best For | Considerations |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Classroom | Complex topics, discussion | Scheduling, location |
| E-learning | Self-paced, wide reach | Engagement, completion |
| Hands-on Labs | Skill building | Environment, time |
| Coaching | Individual support | Resource intensive |
| Job Aids | Quick reference | Design quality |

---

## Training Effectiveness (Kirkpatrick Model)

| Level | Measure | Methods |
|-------|---------|---------|
| 1. Reaction | Satisfaction | Surveys |
| 2. Learning | Knowledge gained | Tests |
| 3. Behavior | Application | Observation |
| 4. Results | Business impact | KPIs |

---

## Key Takeaways

- Training must be role-based and practical
- Multiple delivery methods increase effectiveness
- Evaluation should occur at multiple levels
- Training alone is insufficient - reinforcement is critical
