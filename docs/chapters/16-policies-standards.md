# Chapter 16: Policies, Standards, and Compliance

## Learning Objectives

After completing this chapter, you will be able to:
- Define OCM policies and standards
- Establish compliance requirements
- Implement audit and review processes
- Align with organizational policies

---

## OCM Policy Framework

### Policy 1: OCM Requirement Policy

**Statement**: All projects with significant people impact shall include formal OCM activities proportional to change magnitude.

**Triggers**:
- Projects affecting 50+ users
- Process changes requiring new skills
- System implementations
- Organizational restructuring

---

### Policy 2: Stakeholder Engagement Policy

**Statement**: All impacted stakeholders shall be identified, analyzed, and engaged according to defined standards.

**Requirements**:
- Stakeholder analysis documented
- Engagement plan developed
- Feedback mechanisms established

---

### Policy 3: Communication Standards

**Statement**: Change communications shall follow established standards for content, approval, and delivery.

**Standards**:
- Executive approval for enterprise messages
- Consistent branding and formatting
- Multi-channel delivery
- Feedback collection

---

## Compliance Framework

| Requirement | Evidence | Review Frequency |
|-------------|----------|------------------|
| Stakeholder Analysis | Documented analysis | Project gates |
| Communication Plan | Approved plan | Monthly |
| Training Completion | Completion records | Pre-go-live |
| Adoption Metrics | Dashboard data | Weekly |

---

## Key Takeaways

- Policies ensure consistent OCM application
- Standards provide quality benchmarks
- Compliance frameworks enable oversight
- Regular reviews maintain adherence
