# Chapter 13: Reinforcement and Sustainability

## Learning Objectives

After completing this chapter, you will be able to:
- Design reinforcement mechanisms
- Prevent regression to old behaviors
- Sustain adoption over time
- Transition ownership to the business

---

## Why Reinforcement Matters

Without reinforcement:
- 70% of changes fail to sustain
- Old habits resurface within 6 months
- Benefits erode over time
- Investment is wasted

---

## Reinforcement Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Purpose | Examples |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| **Recognition** | Reward desired behaviors | Awards, celebrations |
| **Accountability** | Ensure compliance | Metrics, reviews |
| **Integration** | Embed in operations | Processes, systems |
| **Support** | Enable continued success | Help desk, coaching |
| **Communication** | Maintain awareness | Success stories, updates |

---

## Sustainability Checklist

- [ ] Performance expectations updated
- [ ] Processes documented and accessible
- [ ] Support structures in place
- [ ] Metrics being tracked
- [ ] Ownership transitioned to business
- [ ] Continuous improvement process established

---

## Key Takeaways

- Reinforcement is essential for lasting change
- Multiple mechanisms work together
- Transition to business ownership ensures sustainability
- Continuous improvement prevents stagnation
